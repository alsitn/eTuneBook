Welcome to eTuneBook


With eTuneBook you can

  • Manage Abc tunes in your browser.
  • Build tune sets.
  • Have a quick look at the first bars of a tune (e.g. on your smart phone during a session).
  • Remember when you last played a tune and how good you were.
  • Label tunes with a color or add a comment.
  • Transpose tunes.
  • Store links to other sites holding tune informations and jump to those sites by clicking the site buttons.
  • Store links to YouTube videos and view them in eTuneBook by clicking the video buttons.
  • Easily identify those tunes you should rehearse. Just sort by last play dates, rehearse frequency or skill.
  • Find tunes by title, key, type or color.

Get started by choosing

  • View > Getting Started

eTuneBook is free of use. If you find it useful, you might consider donations for nature conservation.

Getting started


1. Choose a good Browser

I recommend Chrome. It's by far the fastest Browser and works best with eTuneBook. Firefox would be another option.
I do not recommend other Browsers. They are either dead slow or do not support loading Abc Tunebooks from the local disc.


2. Activate Javascript and local Storage

Normally this stuff is activated by default.
In case you disabled Javascript, you won't see nothing or weird things.
In case you disabled local Storage, you won't be able to save your changes.


3. Get familiar with the abc tune format

eTuneBook uses the abc tune format which is defined at abcnotation.com. There are many sources of abc tunes and abc tunebooks out there. At session.org for example you can find thousands of irish session tunes in abc format.


4. Have a look at the eTuneBook Manual

All eTuneBook functions are explained under View > Manual.


5. Choose from the TuneBook-Menu

  • TuneBook > New: Begin from scratch
  • TuneBook > Open: Import a Tunebook from your local disc
  • TuneBook > Example: Load an example Tunebook from the server

Manual

Import Abc tunes

TuneBook > Open
Import an Abc Tunebook from your local disc.

TuneBook > Example: {{exampleFileName}}
Load an example Tunebook from the server. Current example is BoxPlayer.abc (irish tunes with fingering for piano accordion). For more information about BoxPlayer.abc please visit boxplayer.ch (which will soon be ready)


Add Abc tunes

Copy an Abc-tune to the clipboard , e.g. from session.org. Don't copy the first line which starts with 'X:'. eTuneBook generates the X-line automatically.

TuneBook > New
Generate a new Tunebook with one new tune.

Tune > New
Add a new tune.

In both cases the Abc-Editor for the new Tune opens. Paste the Abc-tune from the clipboard to the Abc-Editor. The dots are shown simultanously on the right side.

The Abc-Editor will be closed as soon as you click somewhere else or change windows.


Export Abc tunes

Every change you make to your Tunebook is immediately saved to the local storage of your browser. You never have to press a save button.

However, for savety reasons you should regularly export Abc tunes:

View > Abc
Displays the Abc-Tunes currently selected. If no selection is active, the whole Tunebook is exported. From the Settings-Menu you further can choose which of the eTuneBook-specific fields should be exported.

Copy the exported Abc-Tunes to the clipboard and paste them to your document-version of your tunebook. eTuneBook cannot do the document-save for you at the moment, because there is no browser-wide support for this.


Edit or delete Abc tunes

The Abc-Code of a tune can be edited by pressing:

Abc-Button
The Abc-Editor opens. The dots are shown simultanously on the right side.
In case you want to get rid of the tune, just delete the whole Abc-Code of the tune.


Build tune sets

In eTuneBook every tune belongs to one or more set. If you load an abc-TuneBook that was generated by another program or by hand, eTuneBook puts every tune in a separate set. Also new tunes are first put into a new set.

In order to build tune sets, tunes are moved around using the Move-Panel. The Move-Panel can be opened/closed by pressing:

Move-Button
The Move-Panel opens. Choose where to move: before or after another tune. After pressing 'Move' on the Move-Panel eTuneBook puts the tune at the chosen position in the target set and closes the Move-Panel. In case the original set gets empty after moving a tune, the empty set will be automatically removed.


Edit tune info

The Info-Editor can be opened/closed by pressing:

Info-Button

  • Change how many times the tunes is repeated in the set (default is 3x).
  • Add up to three links to sites containing additional tune infos. Valid formats are defined by the placeholders.
  • Add up to three links to youTube-videos. Valid formats are defined by the placeholders.
  • Add a comment.

Add rehearse infos

Played-Button
After you played a tune, hit the Played-Button. eTuneBook then stores the playing date. On the base of all playing Dates eTuneBook constantly calculates rehearse frequency scores for sorting tunes.

Skill-Buttons (tumbs-up/thumbs-down):
After you played a tune, judge how good you are by pressing the thumbs-up or -down-Button. eTuneBook then stores your skill judgement.

Color-Selector
Colorize the tune by selecting a color. Colors can have a meaning or not, it's up to you. eTuneBook stores the chosen color.


View the dots

dots-Button
Opens/Closes the dots on the right side of the tune.

Transpose the tune by clicking the arrow up or down. This changes both the abc and the dots.

Fingering can be switched on/off in the settings-menu.


Jump to other sites

The site-Buttons (s1,s2,s3) are displayed, in case the corresponding sites have been specified using the Info-Editor

s1-Button
Opens site 1 in a new window.

s2-Button
Opens site 2 in a new window.

s3-Button
Opens site 3 in a new window.


View videos

The video-Buttons (v1,v2,v3) are displayed, in case the corresponding videos have been specified using the Info-Editor

v1-Button
Opens/closes video 1 on the right side of the tune.

v2-Button
Opens/closes video 2 on the right side of the tune.

v3-Button
Opens/closes video 3 on the right side of the tune.


Filters

Sets can be filtered by:

Sort

Sets can be sorted by:

  • Last Playing Date

  • Rehearse Frequency

  • TuneSet Id

  • Random Order

Sorting direction can be changed by clicking the same menu item again.

Settings

You can show/hide tune-buttons in the TuneSet-View:

  • Rehearse Buttons
    Show/Hide the first line of tune-buttons (Played-Button, Skill-Button, Color-Selector).

  • Info Buttons
    Show/Hide the second line of tune-buttons (Dots-Button, Site-Buttons, YouTube-Buttons).

  • Edit Buttons
    Show/Hide the third line of tune-buttons (Abc-Button, Info-Button, Move-Button).

You can choose between the normal TuneSet-View and a more compact session modus (less scrolling on smaller screens).

You can choose to export eTuneBook-specific fields:

  • TuneSets

  • Playdates

  • Skills

  • Colors

  • Comments

  • Sites

  • Videos

Attention: If you choose to export only standard Abc eTuneBook-specific fields will be lost after Re-Import.

You can switch on/off fingering when viewing dots or Abc-exports.

Limitations / Issues

  • Tunes can only be moved, not copied; A tune cannot exist in more than one set.
  • No printing yet.
  • No Midi Integration.
  • Chords cannot be switched on/off.
  • Performance is bad in other browsers than Chrome.
